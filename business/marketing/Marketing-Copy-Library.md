## 1. Messaging Principles

### Core Promise

> **Help businesses keep customers coming back.**

This is the foundation. Every message — a homepage headline, an ad, an email subject line, a support tooltip — should connect to this idea. If it does not, rewrite it.

### Core Truth

Customers already visited once. Bringing them back is easier and cheaper than finding new ones. OneMember is the tool that makes it happen.

This truth should underpin every piece of copy at every stage of the merchant journey. It is not a slogan. It is a business reality that every merchant intuitively recognises the moment they hear it.

### Tone of Voice

**Plain.** Use the simplest word that communicates the idea. Never use a technical or formal word when a conversational one does the same job.

**Direct.** Say what you mean in the first sentence. Do not bury the point after preamble.

**Benefit-first.** Lead with what the merchant gains. Features come after.

**Warm but not casual.** Friendly and professional. Not corporate. Not overly relaxed.

**Confident without hype.** OneMember knows what it does. State it clearly. Do not exaggerate.

---

### Writing Checklist

Before publishing any marketing message, confirm all five answers are yes:

- [ ] Does this focus on customer retention — not loyalty software?
- [ ] Would a busy merchant understand it immediately, without re-reading?
- [ ] Does it explain a business benefit — not a product feature?
- [ ] Is it simple — would removing a sentence make it better?
- [ ] Is it believable — could every word be defended without qualification?

If any answer is no, rewrite before publishing.

### Benefit Sections

Benefit-led sections that explain what merchants actually get. Each has a heading and a short description.

**More repeat customers**
A customer with rewards in progress is more likely to choose your business over a competitor. OneMember gives your regulars a tangible reason to come back — every single visit.

**Your first real customer list**
Know who your customers are for the first time. Every member has a profile with their full visit history, their current balance, and the last time they came in. No more guessing who your best customers are.

**The digital stamp card that works**
Paper cards get lost. Digital rewards do not. OneMember replaces the paper card your customers keep forgetting at home with a system that remembers everything automatically.

**A loyalty programme that runs itself**
Once your campaign is set up, OneMember handles the tracking. Your team adds members and records purchases — the points, stamps, and rewards happen automatically.

**Insights without the complexity**
See who your most active members are, which campaigns are running, and how many rewards have been redeemed — all on one screen, without needing a data analyst.

---

### Feature Sections

Feature-led sections for visitors who want specifics. Features are still framed around outcomes.

**Points campaigns**
Set a spend-based earn rate. Customers earn points on every purchase and redeem them for rewards you design. Ideal for restaurants, clinics, spas, and retail shops where transaction values vary.

**Stamps campaigns**
One stamp per visit. A reward when the card is complete. The digital version of the paper stamp card your customers already understand — except this one cannot be lost.

**Member profiles**
Every customer gets a profile. Their name, contact details, birthday, visit history, current balance, and your private notes — all in one place. Accessible instantly at the counter.

**Birthday rewards**
Automatically flag when a member's birthday is approaching. Offer a reward they will remember. The kind of personal touch that turns a regular into a loyal advocate.

**Dashboard overview**
One screen shows you everything that matters: active members, campaigns, points issued, rewards redeemed, and your top customers by visit frequency.

Customers do not want another app to download. They do not need one. Your team manages everything from behind the counter. Your customers just give their name — and they're a member.

**Built for independent businesses**
Enterprise loyalty platforms are built for large retail chains with IT teams and six-figure budgets. OneMember is built for the owner-operator who is excellent at running their business and just needs a simple tool to keep customers returning.

**A price that makes sense**
OneMember costs less than one customer visit per day. If it brings back even one additional regular per week, it pays for itself many times over.

## 12. FAQ Copy

These answers are written for use on the website FAQ page, help articles, and sales conversations.

---

**1. How long does it take to set up OneMember?**
The setup wizard takes approximately 10 minutes to complete. By the end, your loyalty programme is live and ready for your first customer.

---

**2. Do my customers need to download an app?**
No. Your customers never need to download anything. You manage the loyalty programme from behind your counter. Customers give you their name or phone number, and you add them to the system. The experience for them is simply: visit, earn, return, redeem.

---

**3. What devices can I use OneMember on?**
OneMember works on any device with a web browser — a smartphone, a tablet, or a computer. No installation required.

---

**4. Can my staff use OneMember?**
Yes. Any team member can add a member, record a purchase, or process a redemption. Adding a staff account means the programme continues running even when you are not at the counter.

---

**5. How do points work?**
You set an earn rate — for example, 1 point for every 100 baht spent. Customers earn points every time a purchase is recorded. When they reach the threshold you set, they can redeem their points for a reward you have configured.

---

**6. How do stamps work?**
Stamps work like a digital version of your paper stamp card. Customers earn one stamp per visit. When they reach the number of stamps you set, they earn a reward. You decide how many stamps are required and what the reward is.

---

**7. What rewards can I offer?**
You design your own rewards. Common examples include a free product, a percentage discount, a complimentary service, a free upgrade, or anything else that is meaningful to your customers and sustainable for your business.

---

**8. Can I run both points and stamps at the same time?**
Yes. You can create multiple campaigns. Some businesses run a stamps campaign for frequent small purchases and a points campaign for larger or less frequent transactions.

---

**9. How much does OneMember cost?**
OneMember offers several subscription plans priced for small businesses. Every new account starts with a free 30-day trial with full access to all features. No credit card is required to start. Visit the pricing page for the current plan details.

---

**10. Can I cancel at any time?**
Yes. There is no contract and no minimum term. You can cancel your subscription at any time. If you cancel, your account moves to the free plan and your data is retained.

---

**11. What happens to my customer data if I cancel?**
Your customer data is always safe regardless of your plan or subscription status. If you cancel or downgrade, nothing is deleted. Your member records, transaction history, and campaign settings are all retained.

---

**12. What happens when my free trial ends?**
If you do not upgrade before the trial ends, your account automatically moves to the free plan. You keep access to the product with free-tier limits. Your member data is always retained.

---

**13. Can I import my existing customer list?**
Contact support to ask about bulk import options for your customer data.

---

**14. Do I need to integrate OneMember with my POS?**
No. OneMember works independently alongside your existing POS or cashier system. There is nothing to integrate. Your team records purchases in OneMember after the sale — it takes 15 seconds.

---

**15. What types of businesses use OneMember?**
OneMember is used by cafés, coffee shops, hair salons, beauty and aesthetic clinics, restaurants, pet grooming salons, gyms, fitness studios, dental clinics, car washes, independent retail shops, and specialty food businesses. It is designed for owner-operated businesses with repeat customers.

---

**16. How many members can I have?**
The number of members depends on your subscription plan. Every plan allows you to add members and grow your programme. See the pricing page for current plan limits.

---

**17. How do birthday rewards work?**
When you add a member, you can record their birthday. OneMember will flag upcoming birthdays so you can issue a reward at the right time. The reward itself is one you design — a free item, a discount, or a complimentary service.

---

**18. Can I change my campaign settings after setting up?**
Yes. You can edit your campaign type, earn rate, stamp count, reward, and other settings at any time. Changes take effect immediately.

---

**19. What if a customer loses their phone or changes their number?**
The member's record is in OneMember — not on their phone. If a customer changes their phone number, you can update their profile at any time. Their history and reward progress are always preserved.

---

**20. Can I run OneMember across multiple locations?**
OneMember is designed for single-location businesses in its current version. Multi-location support is on the roadmap. Contact us to discuss your specific situation.

---

**21. Is my customer data secure?**
Yes. OneMember stores all data securely. Customer contact details are not shared with third parties. See the Privacy Policy for full details.

---

**22. What language is OneMember available in?**
The current version of OneMember is available in English and Thai.

---

**23. Can I offer different rewards on the same campaign?**
Yes. You can add multiple rewards to a campaign at different point or stamp thresholds. For example: a small reward at 5 stamps and a larger reward at 10 stamps.

---

**24. How do I know if my loyalty programme is working?**
Your dashboard shows you active members, purchases recorded, points issued, and rewards redeemed. You can see which members are most active and which campaigns are generating the most engagement.

---

**25. How does a customer redeem a reward?**
The customer tells you they want to redeem a reward at the counter. You look up their profile in OneMember, confirm they have enough points or stamps, and process the redemption. The reward is recorded instantly.

---

**26. Can I pause a campaign?**
Yes. You can pause any campaign at any time. While paused, customers do not earn points or stamps on that campaign. You can resume the campaign whenever you are ready.

---

**27. What happens to earned points or stamps if I change my campaign?**
Existing member balances are retained when you edit a campaign. Members keep their earned points or stamps regardless of changes to the earn rate or reward threshold.

---

**28. Can OneMember send messages to my customers?**
In the current version, OneMember manages the loyalty programme from the merchant side. Customer-facing communications are the merchant's responsibility. Future versions will include customer notification features.

---

**29. Is there a mobile app for merchants?**
OneMember is a web application that works in the browser on any device — including mobile phones. A dedicated mobile app is on the roadmap for a future version.

---

**30. How do I get help if I am stuck?**
Reply to any email from OneMember, use the in-app help, or contact our support team directly. We respond to every enquiry.

## 13. Objection Handling Library

Use these responses in sales conversations, emails, and support interactions. They are written in the OneMember tone — honest, direct, and benefit-focused.

---

### "It's too expensive."

**Actual concern:** The merchant does not yet see the value clearly enough to justify the price. The price is being compared to zero, not to an outcome.

**Response:**
The free 30-day trial means there is no financial commitment to evaluate it. Once you have used it for two weeks — with real members and real purchases — the value becomes clear on its own. Most merchants find that the loyalty programme pays for itself within the first month if it brings back even one customer per week who would otherwise not have returned. Start free and see the numbers for yourself before making any decision.

---

### "I'm too busy."

**Actual concern:** The merchant fears the setup and ongoing management will add to their workload.

**Response:**
Setup takes 10 minutes — once. After that, adding a member takes 30 seconds and recording a purchase takes 15. There is no ongoing management, no reports to run, no settings to review. The system runs automatically once it is set up. The only thing your team does differently is ask customers for their name when they first come in.

---

### "We already use paper stamp cards."

**Actual concern:** The current method feels adequate. Switching is perceived as unnecessary effort.

**Response:**
Paper cards work for the customers who remember to bring them. Most do not. And when a customer loses the card, you lose the record — there is no way to continue their loyalty progress. OneMember keeps every member's progress in the system automatically. No card needed. No lost progress. And for the first time, you have a record of who your most loyal customers actually are.

---

### "I need to get approval from my partner / manager."

**Actual concern:** The decision requires another person's buy-in.

**Response:**
Completely understandable. Here is what might help your conversation: the first 30 days are free, there is no credit card required, and the account can be cancelled at any time. The risk of trying it is essentially zero. If you would find it useful, I can prepare a short summary of how OneMember works for a [business type] specifically — something you can share with your partner before making a decision.

---

### "My customers won't use it / they don't want loyalty programmes."

**Actual concern:** The merchant believes their customer base is not receptive to loyalty rewards.

**Response:**
Your customers do not have to do anything different. They do not download an app. They do not create an account. They just give their name when they first visit — which they already do in many businesses. The loyalty programme runs in the background. Most customers are pleasantly surprised when they are told they have been accumulating rewards.

---

### "We're too small."

**Actual concern:** The merchant believes loyalty programmes are for larger businesses.

**Response:**
The businesses that benefit most from OneMember are small. A customer who visits a boutique café or a local salon three times a week is worth more to that business than any loyalty programme feature. OneMember was built for a business with 50–500 customers who come in regularly — not for large retail chains. If you have 30 regulars and would like more of them to come back more often, OneMember is designed precisely for that.

---

### "It's too complicated."

**Actual concern:** A past experience with software has set an expectation of complexity.

**Response:**
The setup wizard is six steps. The longest step is choosing whether to use points or stamps. After that, adding a member takes 30 seconds and recording a purchase takes 15. There is nothing to configure, nothing to maintain, and nothing to update. If you have 10 minutes, you can have a running loyalty programme before the end of the day.

---

### "We need more features before we can use it."

**Actual concern:** The merchant has specific requirements they believe are not met.

**Response:**
Tell me more about what you are looking for. In most cases, the core loyalty cycle — earning rewards, tracking members, processing redemptions — is already what makes the biggest difference. Features that seem essential before using the product often turn out to be secondary once the programme is running. What specifically would you need?

---

### "Switching from our current system is complicated."

**Actual concern:** Fear of disruption and the effort of changing a working habit.

**Response:**
OneMember does not require you to stop anything you are currently doing. It runs alongside whatever system you have. If you are currently using paper cards, you can run both in parallel while you transition — starting new customers on OneMember and completing existing paper cards in the old system. There is no forced cutover.

---

### "I'm worried about my customer data."

**Actual concern:** Privacy, security, and control over customer information.

**Response:**
Your customer data belongs to you. OneMember stores it securely and does not share it with third parties. If you cancel or downgrade, your data is retained — nothing is deleted. You can request an export of your customer data at any time. Our Privacy Policy details exactly how data is handled.

## 15. Words We Avoid

These terms undermine the brand's promise of simplicity, honesty, and accessibility. Avoid them in all copy.

| Term | Why We Avoid It |
|------|----------------|
| **Revolutionary / Game-changing** | Overused superlatives that signal hype rather than substance. A merchant does not need revolution — they need something that works |
| **Disruptive** | Technology industry jargon that means nothing to a café owner or salon operator |
| **Enterprise-grade** | OneMember is not positioned as enterprise software. Using this term contradicts the brand's promise of simplicity and accessibility |
| **AI-powered** | Avoid unless a specific AI feature is being described directly. A general "AI-powered loyalty platform" claim is vague and invites scepticism |
| **Guaranteed** | No product can guarantee business outcomes. Using this word creates legal exposure and breaks trust when results vary |
| **Best in the world / #1** | Superlatives require proof. Without it, they read as self-promotion without substance |
| **Unlimited profits / revenue** | Exaggerated outcome claims. OneMember helps bring customers back — it does not guarantee financial results |
| **Solution** | The most overused word in B2B software. "OneMember is a loyalty solution" says nothing. "OneMember keeps customers coming back" says everything |
| **Leverage** | Corporate jargon. Replace with "use" in every case |
| **Synergy** | Does not belong in copy written for small business owners |
| **Robust** | Vague qualifier. Describe what it actually does instead |
| **Seamless** | Overused in SaaS. Describe the actual experience instead |
| **Holistic** | Meaningless in context. Replace with a specific description of what the product covers |
| **Scalable** | Relevant for enterprise audiences. Not relevant for a café owner who needs something that works today |
| **User** | Impersonal. In external copy, use "merchant," "you," or "your customers" |
| **Customer journey** | Internal term. Do not use in merchant-facing copy |
| **Onboarding** | Internal term. In merchant-facing copy, say "setup" or "getting started" |
| **Platform** | Borderline. Acceptable in formal descriptions but avoid in conversational copy |
| **Ecosystem** | Enterprise jargon. Avoid entirely |
| **Next-gen / Next-generation** | Meaningless marketing filler |
| **Cutting-edge** | Same as above. Replace with a specific description of what makes it useful |
