# 13 — Industry Strategy

This document provides industry-specific guidance for OneMember's ten supported target industries.

It is informational only. It does not define any industry-specific functionality or database schema. All merchants use the same platform regardless of industry.

This document is used to:
- Guide the product team when designing onboarding templates.
- Inform copy and marketing materials per industry.
- Help merchants understand how OneMember applies to their business.

---

## 1. Hair Salons

### Typical Business Goals
- Increase repeat visits from existing clients.
- Reward clients who spend consistently over time.
- Retain clients who might otherwise visit a competitor.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points per baht spent** — e.g. earn 1 point per 10 THB; redeem for free treatments.
- **Stamp card** — e.g. visit 10 times, get the 11th treatment at 50% off.
- **Birthday bonus** — extra points credited in the client's birthday month.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- Free haircut (redeemable after reaching a points threshold).
- 20% discount on colouring or treatment services.
- Free conditioning treatment with any cut.
- Priority booking (future feature, informational only).

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- Clients visit regularly (every 4–8 weeks).
- They have a preferred stylist and are loyal to the salon when rewarded.
- Price sensitivity is moderate; perceived value of the reward matters more than monetary value.
- Birthday acknowledgement has high emotional impact.

## 2. Nail Salons

### Typical Business Goals
- Drive consistent repeat visits (clients typically visit every 2–4 weeks).
- Differentiate from nearby competitors offering similar services at similar prices.
- Upsell additional services (nail art, treatments) through rewards.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Stamp card** — e.g. 8 visits = 1 free basic manicure.
- **Points per service value** — higher-value services earn proportionally more points.
- **Seasonal promotions** — bonus stamps during festive periods.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- Free nail art design on next visit.
- Complimentary gel removal with any new set.
- 15% discount on any premium service.
- Free hand treatment with any manicure.

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- High visit frequency makes stamp cards particularly effective.
- Customers often bring friends; referral potential is high (future feature).
- Social sharing of nail designs is common; rewards can be tied to reviews (future feature).

## 3. Massage & Spa

### Typical Business Goals
- Convert first-time visitors into regular clients.
- Increase average spend per visit (upsell to longer sessions or premium treatments).
- Fill off-peak appointment slots.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points program** — earn points based on session length or service value.
- **Stamp card for signature treatment** — e.g. 5 visits to the signature massage = 1 free session.
- **Birthday reward** — complimentary upgrade to a longer session during birthday month.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- 30-minute session extension at no charge.
- Free aromatherapy add-on with any 60-minute booking.
- Complimentary birthday massage (30 minutes).
- 10% discount on a series package.

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- Visits are less frequent than nail salons (every 2–6 weeks).
- Customers are willing to travel for a trusted therapist.
- Relaxation and wellness positioning resonates; rewards should feel indulgent, not transactional.

## 4. Restaurants & Cafés

### Typical Business Goals
- Increase visit frequency among regulars.
- Grow average spend per visit.
- Compete with large chain loyalty programmes.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points per baht spent** — straightforward earn on every bill.
- **Stamp card for a specific item** — e.g. buy 9 coffees, get the 10th free.
- **Birthday reward** — free dessert or complimentary drink during birthday month.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- Free coffee or soft drink after stamp card completion.
- 50 THB discount voucher on any bill over 300 THB.
- Free birthday dessert.
- Complimentary upgrade to large size on any beverage.

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- High visit frequency (daily for coffee, weekly for dining).
- Stamp cards work particularly well for single-item repeat purchases (coffee, set lunch).
- Price sensitivity is higher than salons; cashback-style rewards resonate well.
- Mobile convenience matters — members want quick identification at the counter.

## 5. Hotels

### Typical Business Goals
- Reward repeat guests and corporate travellers.
- Increase ancillary spend (restaurant, spa, room upgrades).
- Compete with large hotel chain loyalty programmes for independent properties.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points per night stayed** — earn points for each night booked directly.
- **Spend-based program** — points on all hotel spend (room, F&B, spa combined).
- **Birthday reward** — complimentary room upgrade or late checkout during birthday stay.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- Free room upgrade (subject to availability).
- Complimentary airport transfer.
- Late checkout extension.
- Free breakfast for two.
- Points redeemable toward future nights.

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- Visit frequency is lower but transaction value is much higher.
- Points programs suit hotels better than stamp cards due to variable spend.
- Business travellers prioritise convenience; leisure guests prioritise experiences.
- Loyalty programmes can directly influence direct booking vs. OTA booking decisions.

## 6. Fashion Retail

### Typical Business Goals
- Increase repeat purchase frequency.
- Drive full-price purchases rather than waiting for sales.
- Build an engaged customer base to support new collection launches.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points per baht spent** — straightforward points on every purchase.
- **New season bonus** — double points during the first week of a new collection.
- **Birthday reward** — exclusive early access or voucher during birthday month.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- 100 THB store credit redeemable on next purchase.
- Exclusive member discount on new arrivals.
- Free alterations with any purchase over a threshold.
- Gift wrapping service at no charge.

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- Purchase frequency varies (1–4 times per year for fashion, more for accessories).
- Customers are price-aware; percentage discounts resonate more than free items.
- Seasonal buying patterns are strong; rewards timed around key seasons drive behaviour.
- Brand affinity plays a significant role in loyalty programme engagement.

## 7. Beauty & Cosmetics

### Typical Business Goals
- Encourage repeat purchase of consumable products.
- Cross-sell across product categories.
- Reward high-value customers who purchase premium lines.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points per baht spent** — applied across all product categories.
- **Category bonus** — extra points on skincare or a highlighted product line.
- **Birthday reward** — complimentary product sample or discount during birthday month.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- Free full-size product after reaching a points threshold.
- Sample bundle redeemable with any purchase.
- 15% discount on a specific product category.
- Complimentary skin consultation.

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- Repurchase cycle for consumables is regular (1–3 months).
- Customers are highly brand-loyal within a category once they find products they like.
- Trial samples are effective in driving full-size purchases — rewards that introduce new products build long-term value.
- Online and in-store shopping may be mixed; points should be consistent regardless of channel (future consideration).

## 8. Grocery Stores

### Typical Business Goals
- Increase basket size per visit.
- Drive visit frequency versus competitors.
- Reward loyal shoppers who could otherwise choose a large supermarket chain.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points per baht spent** — applied to the total bill.
- **Product category bonus** — extra points on fresh produce, bakery, or a weekly featured category.
- **Milestone reward** — bonus points when cumulative monthly spend crosses a threshold.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- 50 THB discount on any bill over 500 THB.
- Free branded tote bag after reaching a points level.
- Complimentary delivery on next order (if delivery is offered).
- Priority queue at checkout (operational reward, no cost to merchant).

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- Very high visit frequency (weekly or more).
- Basket value varies; large baskets earn faster, which motivates higher spend.
- Points programs suit grocery better than stamp cards due to variable basket size.
- Price is the dominant factor; rewards must deliver clear monetary value.

## 9. Pet Shops

### Typical Business Goals
- Retain pet owners as long-term customers across the pet's lifetime.
- Drive repeat purchases of consumable products (food, accessories).
- Reward customers who spend on services (grooming, vet check-ins).

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points per baht spent** — on all purchases including food and accessories.
- **Grooming stamp card** — e.g. 6 grooming sessions = 1 free.
- **Birthday reward** — a birthday reward for the pet's birthday (tracks pet birthday rather than owner's).

### Recommended Reward Examples
- Free bag of pet food after accumulating sufficient points.
- Complimentary grooming session after stamp card completion.
- 20% discount on a specific pet food brand.
- Free pet birthday treat with any purchase during the pet's birthday month.

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- Extremely high loyalty once a pet owner trusts a shop.
- Consumable repurchases (food, litter, treats) drive high visit frequency.
- Emotional attachment to the pet extends to the brand that serves them — birthday acknowledgements for pets have strong emotional resonance.
- Grooming services create a reliable recurring appointment pattern.

> **Note:** Pet birthday tracking would require an additional data field not currently in the members table. This is a future consideration only.

## 10. Wholesale Businesses

### Typical Business Goals
- Retain business customers (B2B) who place regular bulk orders.
- Reward volume purchases.
- Differentiate from online wholesale platforms through service and loyalty.

### Recommended Campaign Examples
- **Points per baht spent** — applied to order value.
- **Volume tier bonus** — extra points when a single order exceeds a threshold amount.
- **Annual loyalty reward** — significant reward issued at the end of the year based on cumulative spend.

### Recommended Reward Examples
- Percentage discount on next bulk order.
- Free delivery on next order over a threshold.
- Priority fulfilment during peak periods.
- Dedicated account manager access (operational reward).

### Typical Customer Behaviour
- Purchase frequency is lower but transaction values are significantly higher.
- B2B customers are highly rational; rewards must have clear business value, not lifestyle appeal.
- Relationship and reliability matter more than the loyalty programme itself — the programme reinforces an existing relationship rather than creating loyalty from scratch.
- Annual reporting on their points balance and rewards earned supports their own business accounting.

> **Note:** B2B loyalty programmes may require invoice-based transaction recording rather than point-of-sale capture. This is a future consideration only.
