# Merchant Sales Playbook
## OneMember — MKTG-002 | Confidential | 2026

---

## Purpose

This playbook is for anyone selling OneMember to a merchant. It covers the full sales journey — from first contact to signed account — using a persuasion-first, outcome-focused approach.

The objective is not to explain the product. The objective is to get the merchant to sign up.

---

## Mindset

You are not selling software. You are solving a business problem that every merchant has but few can name.

> "Your customers are forgetting you — and you're losing ฿48,000 a month because of it."

Merchants respond to revenue, not features. Lead with the pain. Show the cost. Then show the fix.

---

## Target Merchant Profile

### Best-fit merchants:
- **Cafés and restaurants** — high repeat-visit potential, cash-heavy, underserved by loyalty tech
- **Fitness studios and salons** — subscription-adjacent, member mindset, personal relationships
- **Boutique retail** — fashion, wellness, lifestyle — aspirational brands with loyal regulars
- **Food & beverage chains** — 2–5 locations, owner-operator, no enterprise tools

### Qualification criteria:
| Criterion | Minimum |
|---|---|
| Weekly customer footfall | 50+ |
| Average transaction value | ฿150+ |
| Owner or manager present in meeting | Required |
| Interest in customer retention | Must be a pain point |

### Disqualify if:
- Business is closing or relocating within 3 months
- Owner has no customer-facing time (cannot promote the programme)
- Currently using a competing loyalty platform with long contract

## Sales Stages

### Stage 1 — Discovery (Before the meeting)

**Goal:** Understand the business before walking in.

**What to find out:**
- Business type and location
- Estimated daily customer volume
- Current loyalty programme (if any)
- Who is the decision maker?

**Tools:** Google Maps, Instagram, or a quick visit as a customer first.

**Time required:** 15 minutes.

---

### Stage 2 — Opening (First 5 minutes)

**Goal:** Establish credibility and earn the right to present.

**Script:**
> "Thank you for your time. I'm not here to give you a long presentation about software. I want to show you one number — and if that number doesn't concern you, we're done in 10 minutes. Deal?"

This opening creates curiosity and signals respect for their time.

**Then move immediately to Slide 3 (The Cost Calculator).**

---

### Stage 3 — Problem (Slides 1–4)

**Goal:** Make the merchant feel the cost of inaction.

**Key moments:**
1. **The hook (Slide 1):** Let them read it. Don't speak.
2. **The stats (Slide 2):** Present each one. Ask "Does that surprise you?" after each.
3. **The calculator (Slide 3):** Ask their numbers. Fill in the equation. Reveal the result.
4. **The pivot (Slide 4):** Don't answer the question. Just let it sit.

**Listen for:** Signs of recognition — "actually that's true", "I never thought about it that way", a quiet nod.

**Do not rush this section.** This is where the sale is made.

---

### Stage 4 — Solution (Slides 5–7)

**Goal:** Show the product as the logical answer to their specific pain.

**Key messages:**
- "Five outcomes, not five features."
- "Your customers join in 30 seconds. No app."
- "You set it up once. It runs automatically."

**Do not go into technical detail.** If they ask, say "I'll show you exactly how that works in 5 minutes when we set it up together."

---

### Stage 5 — Proof (Slides 8–9)

**Goal:** Give them permission to believe.

**Case studies (Slide 8):** Ask "Which of these sounds most like your business?" and focus on that story.

**ROI Calculator (Slide 9):** Personalise with their numbers. Show the 22× ROI. Hand over the physical calculator.

> "For every ฿1 you spend on OneMember, you get ฿22 back. The payback period is under two weeks."

---

### Stage 6 — Trust + Urgency (Slides 10–11)

**Goal:** Remove doubt and create a reason to act today.

**Trust (Slide 10):** Lead with "You own your data." Emphasise PDPA compliance only if they ask about it — otherwise keep this slide to 30 seconds.

**Urgency (Slide 11):** Do not apply pressure. Apply logic.
> "Every week you wait is a week of data you don't have. The merchants who start first own the loyalty advantage."

---

### Stage 7 — Close (Slides 12–13)

**Goal:** Get an account opened before you leave.

**Risk reversal first (Slide 12):**
> "No contract. No setup fee. 30-day refund if it doesn't work. What do you have to lose?"

**The ask (Slide 13):**
> "Let's open your account right now. It takes 5 minutes. I'll walk you through it."

**Hard ask:** "Can I have your phone for 30 seconds? I'll start the registration for you."

## Handling the Meeting

### Who should be in the room?

Always: the business owner or the person who signs off on expenses.

If you're meeting a staff member: "I'd love to include the owner in this conversation — is there a time this week when they're free for 20 minutes?"

### Meeting length

| Phase | Time |
|---|---|
| Opening | 2 min |
| Problem | 8 min |
| Solution | 5 min |
| Proof + ROI | 5 min |
| Close | 5 min |
| **Total** | **~25 min** |

### What to leave behind

- Printed ROI Calculator (with their numbers filled in)
- Merchant FAQ (one-pager)
- Business card

---

## After the Meeting

### If they signed up:
- Send confirmation email within 1 hour
- Schedule onboarding call within 48 hours
- Check in after their first 7 days to confirm first member enrolled

### If they said "I'll think about it":
- Set a specific callback date before you leave — "Can I follow up on Thursday at 2pm?"
- Send the ROI Calculator and FAQ within 2 hours
- Follow up exactly on the date agreed. Do not follow up early.

### If they said no:
- Ask: "Is there anything specific that held you back?" Note the objection.
- Send the FAQ with a note: "If anything changes, we're happy to help."
- Log the objection in the sales tracker.

## Competitive Situations

### "We already have a stamp card."

> "Paper stamp cards are a great start — you just can't do anything with them. You can't reach those customers when you're quiet on a Tuesday. With OneMember, every stamp becomes a relationship you can act on."

### "We use [other app]."

Ask: "What do you like about it?" and "What's missing?"

Then position OneMember on the gap, not on price.

### "Our customers don't use apps."

> "Neither do OneMember members — they scan a QR code. No download. No registration. Just a tap."
