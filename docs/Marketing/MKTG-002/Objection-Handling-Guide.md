## How to Use This Guide

Every objection is a question in disguise. The merchant is not saying "no" — they are saying "I'm not convinced yet." Your job is to understand the real concern and address it directly.

**Structure for every objection:**
1. Acknowledge — don't argue
2. Clarify — make sure you understand the real concern
3. Respond — give the honest, direct answer
4. Bridge — move back toward the close

## Price Objections

---

### "It's too expensive."

**What they mean:** I'm not sure the value is worth ฿1,990/month.

**Acknowledge:** "I understand — every baht you spend on your business has to earn its place."

**Respond:**
> "Let me put this in context. Based on your numbers — [X customers, ฿Y average spend] — OneMember generates about ฿[Z]/month in additional revenue from returning customers. That's a 22× return. ฿1,990 is the cost of a few coffees a day to bring back customers who've already chosen you once."

**Bridge:** "Does that change how you're thinking about the investment?"

### "I can't afford it right now."

**What they mean:** Cash flow is tight / I'm not sure it'll pay off.

**Acknowledge:** "That's a real concern and I respect it."

**Respond:**
> "The 30-day guarantee exists exactly for situations like this. You pay ฿1,990. If you don't see your investment returned within the first month, we refund it. You have no downside. And in the meantime, you're building a member database that you keep forever — even if you later decide to leave."

**Bridge:** "Would it help if I showed you the specific number of new return visits you'd need to break even?"

---

### "A stamp card is cheaper."

**What they mean:** I can achieve the same thing for less.

**Acknowledge:** "Paper stamp cards have worked for a long time. They're simple."

**Respond:**
> "The difference is what you can do with the data. A stamp card can't tell you that Customer X hasn't been in for 45 days. It can't send them a birthday offer. It can't show you that your Tuesday afternoon is 40% lower than Thursday. OneMember doesn't replace the loyalty idea — it turns it into a tool you can actually use."

**Bridge:** "What do you do today when a regular customer stops coming in?"

(Let them answer. Usually: "I don't know." That's your answer.)

## Time and Effort Objections

---

### "I don't have time to manage this."

**What they mean:** I'm already overwhelmed running the business.

**Acknowledge:** "You're running a business — the last thing you need is another thing to manage."

**Respond:**
> "OneMember is specifically built for business owners who don't have time. You set it up once. After that, the birthday rewards go out automatically. The win-back campaigns go out automatically. Customers join automatically when they scan. You open your dashboard once a week to see how it's going — that's 10 minutes. Everything else runs itself."

**Bridge:** "If I could show you that managing this takes less time than checking your phone in the morning, would that change things?"

---

### "My staff won't use it."

**What they mean:** I'm worried about training and adoption.

**Acknowledge:** "Staff adoption is a real challenge with any new system."

**Respond:**
> "The good news is that your staff don't need to do much. Customers scan their own QR code. Points are recorded automatically. The only thing staff need to do is point to the QR code and say 'scan this to join our loyalty programme.' That's one sentence."

**Bridge:** "Would it help to do a 5-minute run-through with your team when I set up your account today?"

---

**What they mean:** I'm burned from a past experience and skeptical this is different.

**Acknowledge:** "I'm sorry to hear that — it's frustrating to invest time and money in something that doesn't deliver."

**Respond:** "Can I ask what happened? What specifically didn't work?"

(Listen carefully. Then address the specific failure point.)

**Bridge:** "Based on what you've described, that sounds like [specific problem]. Here's how OneMember handles that differently..."

## Technology Objections

---

### "My customers don't use their phones like that."

**What they mean:** My demographic is older / less tech-savvy.

**Acknowledge:** "That's a fair point — not every customer is a digital native."

**Respond:**
> "All they need to do is point their camera at a QR code. Most people do this every day — for menus, for payments, for social media. And for customers who really don't want to use their phone, you can still run a physical stamp card alongside the programme. But in our experience, even customers who say 'I don't use technology' scan the code when they're standing in front of it."

**Bridge:** "Would you be willing to try it for 30 days and see what your actual adoption rate is? The data might surprise you."

---

### "We already have LINE OA for customer messaging."

**What they mean:** I have an existing communication channel.

**Acknowledge:** "LINE OA is great for broadcasting — a lot of businesses use it."

**Respond:**
> "OneMember and LINE OA solve different problems. LINE OA is for anyone who follows your account — including people who've never visited and may never come. OneMember is built on verified customer visit data. When you send a win-back message through OneMember, you're reaching customers who have actually spent money with you, based on exactly how long it's been since they visited. That's a fundamentally different kind of message — and it converts at a much higher rate."

**Bridge:** "Do you have a way right now to know which LINE followers are actual customers versus people who just followed you once?"

## Timing Objections

---

### "Let me think about it."

**What they mean:** I'm not convinced yet / I want to talk to my partner / I'm avoiding a decision.

**Acknowledge:** "Of course — this is a real business decision."

**Clarify first:** "Can I ask — is there a specific concern you'd like to think through? I want to make sure you have everything you need to make the right call."

(Listen. Address the specific concern if one emerges.)

**If no specific concern:**
> "Here's what I'd suggest: let's open your account now while I'm here — it takes 5 minutes and costs nothing to set up. You can take the 30 days to evaluate it properly, and if it doesn't work, you get your money back. You don't have to decide today whether it's worth ฿1,990. You have to decide today whether it's worth 5 minutes."

**Bridge:** "Can we do those 5 minutes now?"

---

### "Can we start next month?"

**What they mean:** I want to delay the decision.

**Acknowledge:** "Starting next month is fine — we'll be here."

**Respond (with genuine urgency, not pressure):**
> "The only thing I'd say is: every week you wait is a week of customer data you don't have. The 50 customers who come in this week? Next month, you won't know who they were or how to reach them. That data doesn't retroactively appear when you sign up later. Starting next month means you lose this month's relationships."

**Bridge:** "What if we at least open your account today and put your QR code on your counter? You don't have to run a campaign until you're ready. But the members start joining now."

---

### "We're too busy right now — we have a big event next week."

**What they mean:** Poor timing / distracted.

**Acknowledge:** "A busy week is actually a perfect time to have OneMember running."

**Respond:**
> "If you have 200 customers coming through your door next week for a special event — that's 200 people you could add to your member database this week instead of losing. Special events are when loyalty programmes shine, not when they should be on pause."

**Bridge:** "What if we set it up in 10 minutes now so it's running for your event?"

### "I've never heard of OneMember."

**What they mean:** You're an unknown brand — I don't know if I can trust you.

**Acknowledge:** "We're a young company — I won't pretend otherwise."

**Respond:**
> "Which is why we offer a 30-day money-back guarantee, no contract, and month-to-month billing. We don't ask you to trust us based on reputation. We ask you to try us based on results. If we don't deliver, you pay nothing."

## Closing Notes

**The objection you can't overcome:** A merchant who genuinely cannot afford to spend ฿1,990 right now. Don't force it. Leave the FAQ, book a follow-up in 60 days, and move on.

**The objection that always closes:** When you personalise the ROI Calculator with their actual numbers. ฿45,000 of additional monthly revenue versus ฿1,990 is a conversation most merchants can have easily.

**The objection that keeps recurring:** "I'll think about it" almost always means "I need more information or I'm not confident." Ask the clarifying question and address the real concern.
