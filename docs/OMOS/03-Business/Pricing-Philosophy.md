## Purpose

This document defines how OneMember thinks about pricing — not what the prices are (those are in the Revenue Model), but the principles that guide pricing decisions.

---

## Core Pricing Beliefs

### 1. Price must reflect value, not cost.

OneMember's pricing is based on the value it delivers to merchants, not on the cost to run the platform. A merchant who gains 50,000 THB in incremental revenue from repeat customers will pay 1,500 THB/month happily. A merchant who gains nothing will not pay anything, regardless of how low the price is.

This means: pricing is secondary to value delivery. If merchants are churning, investigate the product before reducing the price.

### 2. Small businesses must be able to afford us.

The Starter plan must be priced within the discretionary budget of a cafe owner or small salon. If a merchant has to think for more than a few seconds about whether they can afford it, the price is too high for that segment.

Target: Starter plan should feel like "a cost of 2 coffees per day."

### 3. Pricing should reward merchant growth.

As a merchant grows their member base and increases their loyalty activity, they naturally move to higher-tier plans. The tier limits (member count, campaign count, advanced features) should be set so that a merchant who is getting real value from OneMember will want to upgrade — not because we are restricting them artificially, but because the upgrade makes their programme more powerful.

### 4. Annual pricing rewards commitment.

Merchants who commit to an annual subscription receive a discount (typically 2 months free / ~17% discount). This reduces churn and provides revenue predictability. Annual pricing is always presented as the recommended option, with monthly available as a flexibility option.

### 5. No surprise pricing.

Merchants must never be surprised by a charge they did not understand was coming. All pricing is transparent on the pricing page. Plan limits are clearly communicated. Upgrades are optional and prompted clearly. Overages generate a notification before any charge, not after.

### 6. Free trial, not freemium.

OneMember does not offer a permanent free tier. A free tier attracts merchants who are not committed to the platform and creates a support burden for merchants who generate no revenue.

Instead: a time-limited trial (14–30 days) on the Growth plan. After trial: choose a paid plan or stop using OneMember. No "free forever" tier.

Exception: Enterprise demos may be extended on a case-by-case basis by the Product Owner.

### 7. Price increases are communicated early and implemented with respect.

When OneMember needs to increase prices (e.g., as new features are added to existing plans), existing customers receive at least 30 days' notice, the rationale is explained honestly, and a grandfathering period is offered where possible.

Merchants who feel surprised or disrespected by a price increase are merchants who churn. Transparent communication about pricing changes is a trust issue, not just a commercial one.

## Pricing Anti-Patterns We Avoid

- **Feature-gating for spite:** Do not put core features behind the highest plan just to force upgrades. If a feature is fundamental to the loyalty programme, it belongs on the Starter plan.
- **Pricing that punishes loyalty:** Existing customers should never pay more than new customers for the same plan.
- **Opaque enterprise pricing:** "Contact us for pricing" creates friction. Where possible, publish enterprise starting prices.
- **Usage-based billing for merchants:** Charging per transaction or per member (rather than per tier) creates anxiety for merchants about costs scaling unpredictably. Tier-based pricing with clear limits is preferable.
