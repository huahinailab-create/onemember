## Purpose

Customer Personas define the types of consumers who use OneMember-powered loyalty programmes. They guide the design of the member experience (Phase 1 join flow, wallet UX in Phase 2) and help merchants understand who they are rewarding.

---

## Persona 1 — The Local Regular

**Profile:** A person who visits the same small set of nearby merchants repeatedly — the neighbourhood cafe, the lunch restaurant near work, the salon they trust.

**Age:** 25–45  
**Location:** Urban Thailand  
**Devices:** Smartphone (Android or iOS)  
**Loyalty Behaviour:** Uses 2–4 loyalty programmes actively; carries 3–8 paper cards rarely

**Motivations:**
- Wants to be recognised and rewarded by places they already love
- Will not download an app just for one merchant
- Responds well to birthday treats and "welcome back" messages
- Wants to feel like a VIP without having to ask for special treatment

**Pain Points:**
- Paper stamp cards get lost or forgotten at home
- Signing up for each loyalty programme separately is annoying
- Too many apps — doesn't want to install another one
- Points that expire before they can be used

**Phase 1 Experience:** Scans QR at counter. Enters name and phone number. Done in 20 seconds. Earns stamps or points on every visit. Redeems a free drink after 10 stamps.

**Phase 2 (Wallet) Experience:** Opens OneMember wallet app. All 4 of their loyalty memberships visible in one place. Checks points before deciding which cafe to visit.

**What Makes This Persona Love OneMember:** "I don't have to carry cards anymore, and I actually got a birthday message from my favourite cafe."

## Persona 2 — The Office Worker

**Profile:** Works in a commercial area with many food and beverage options. Eats lunch out every day. Has strong preferences for 3–5 lunch spots that rotate.

**Age:** 22–40  
**Location:** Bangkok CBD or commercial district  
**Devices:** Smartphone  
**Loyalty Behaviour:** Actively uses convenience-based loyalty (7-Eleven, food delivery apps); traditional loyalty programmes feel too much effort

**Motivations:**
- Values speed and convenience above all else
- Earns rewards passively — does not want to think about it
- Chooses where to eat based partly on accumulated points
- Responds to "earn double points today" promotions

**Pain Points:**
- Loyalty sign-up forms that take more than 30 seconds
- Having to remember a loyalty card number
- Points that are hard to understand or calculate
- Promotional emails from merchants they barely remember

**Phase 1 Experience:** Scans QR at the restaurant counter while waiting to order. Phone number is saved — no app required. Points accumulate invisibly. Notified by SMS or LINE when enough points for a free meal.

**What Makes This Persona Love OneMember:** "I didn't even think about it and suddenly I had enough points for a free lunch."

## Persona 3 — The Loyalty Enthusiast

**Profile:** Actively manages multiple loyalty programmes. Knows their points balance across programmes. Makes purchase decisions based on loyalty value. Downloads merchant apps willingly.

**Age:** 28–50  
**Location:** Urban, mid-to-high income  
**Devices:** Smartphone, tablet  
**Loyalty Behaviour:** Tracks 8–15 loyalty programmes; uses loyalty apps regularly; understands points-to-value conversion

**Motivations:**
- Maximises value from every purchase
- Enjoys the gamification of loyalty
- Wants transparency: exactly how many points, exactly what they're worth, when they expire
- Interested in the Customer Wallet as a single dashboard for all programmes

**Pain Points:**
- Points that expire without warning
- Rewards that are too hard to redeem
- Opaque points values ("10,000 points for a ฿50 discount?")
- Loyalty programmes with no digital record — paper only

**Phase 1 Experience:** Enthusiastic adopter. Scans every QR they see. Asks staff about their loyalty programme. May become a OneMember ambassador in online communities.

**Phase 2 (Wallet) Experience:** Power user of the wallet. Checks multiple balances. Chooses merchants based on points optimisation. Refers friends.

**What Makes This Persona Love OneMember:** "I can finally see all my loyalty balances in one place. And these points actually have value."

## Persona 4 — The Family Shopper

**Profile:** Primary household shopper. Visits the same grocery store, bakery, pharmacy, and kids' clothing stores regularly. Loyalty is about saving money for the family.

**Age:** 30–55  
**Location:** Suburban, family-focused  
**Devices:** Smartphone (may share with family)  
**Loyalty Behaviour:** Uses supermarket loyalty programmes actively; less engaged with boutique or restaurant programmes

**Motivations:**
- Every discount matters — family budget is a priority
- Loyal to merchants who make their life easier
- Values family-oriented rewards (kids eat free, family discount)
- Wants a simple, reliable experience — not complicated apps

**Pain Points:**
- Sharing points within a family is impossible in most systems
- Programmes that require spending a lot before any reward
- Complicated redemption processes
- Loyalty programmes that don't work at the branch near their home

**Phase 1 Experience:** Joins a loyalty programme at their favourite grocery or bakery. Earns stamps on every visit. Stamps are visible and understandable — 10 stamps, free bread.

**What Makes This Persona Love OneMember:** "My kids love coming with me to the bakery because they know when we've almost got our free bread."

## Persona 5 — The Tourist or Visitor

**Profile:** Visiting Thailand (domestic or international tourist). May visit a merchant 1–3 times during their stay. Will not download a local app. May not speak Thai.

**Age:** Any  
**Location:** Visiting Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Phuket, or other tourist areas  
**Devices:** Smartphone (with data)  
**Loyalty Behaviour:** Rarely uses local loyalty programmes while travelling

**Motivations:**
- Open to a loyalty programme if the sign-up is fast and frictionless
- Will not commit to a long-term relationship with a Thai merchant
- Values instant rewards over long-term accumulation

**Pain Points:**
- Sign-up forms in Thai that they cannot read
- Needing a Thai phone number
- Points they will never be able to redeem because they are leaving

**Phase 1 Considerations:** OneMember must allow sign-up with international phone numbers or email. The join flow must be language-adaptive. For tourist-heavy merchants, instant rewards (complete 2 visits, get a discount on the third) are more valuable than long-term programmes.

**Phase 2 (Wallet) Consideration:** If the wallet supports a universal profile, tourists who return to Thailand can resume their membership from any device.

**What Makes This Persona Engage:** "I scanned the QR code out of curiosity and got a 10% discount on my second visit the same day."

## Customer Persona Priority for Phase 1

| Persona | Phase 1 Priority | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| The Local Regular | **Critical** | Core user of the loyalty programme |
| The Office Worker | **High** | Volume driver for F&B merchants |
| The Loyalty Enthusiast | **Medium** | Brand advocate and wallet early adopter |
| The Family Shopper | **Medium** | Drives retail and bakery merchant value |
| The Tourist / Visitor | **Low** | Edge case — needs multi-language support first |
