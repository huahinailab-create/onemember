## Purpose

Merchant Personas define the types of businesses OneMember serves. They are used to guide product decisions, ensure features serve real merchant needs, and communicate the target market to the team.

When evaluating a feature, ask: which persona does this serve? If it does not clearly serve at least one persona, reconsider whether it belongs in the product.

---

## Persona 1 — The Neighbourhood Cafe

**Business Type:** Food and Beverage — Independent Cafe  
**Size:** 1 location, 2–6 staff  
**Monthly Customers:** 300–800  
**Current Loyalty:** Paper stamp cards or nothing  
**Tech Sophistication:** Low — uses smartphone but no POS software  
**Monthly Loyalty Budget:** 500–1,000 THB  

**Goals:**
- Turn first-time visitors into regulars
- Reward their best customers (the ones who come 3+ times a week)
- Compete with chain cafes without enterprise marketing budgets

**Pain Points:**
- Paper stamp cards get lost, stolen, or exchanged fraudulently
- No way to know which customers are regulars vs. one-time visitors
- Cannot reach customers who have drifted away
- No time or budget for marketing campaigns

**What They Need from OneMember:**
- Setup in one afternoon — not days
- A QR code to display at the counter
- To know who their regulars are and how often they visit
- An automatic birthday treat for loyal customers
- A WhatsApp or LINE-style simple interface — not a corporate dashboard

**Success Metric:** "My regulars tell me they love getting my birthday message."

## Persona 2 — The Growing Restaurant

**Business Type:** Food and Beverage — Restaurant  
**Size:** 1–3 locations, 10–40 staff  
**Monthly Customers:** 1,000–5,000  
**Current Loyalty:** LineOA or basic QR loyalty app  
**Tech Sophistication:** Medium — has a POS system, uses LINE for marketing  
**Monthly Loyalty Budget:** 1,500–3,000 THB  

**Goals:**
- Increase repeat visit frequency
- Grow delivery orders alongside dine-in
- Build a recognisable brand in a competitive market
- Measure which promotions are actually working

**Pain Points:**
- LINE OA delivers messages but cannot measure whether they drive visits
- No link between who received a promotion and who redeemed it
- Multi-branch management is chaotic — different staff doing different things
- Data is scattered: LINE followers, Instagram followers, delivery platform customers are all separate

**What They Need from OneMember:**
- Campaign analytics: did this promotion increase covers?
- Multi-branch loyalty: a customer's points work at all their locations
- Automated campaigns: birthday offers, win-back messages for customers who haven't visited in 60 days
- Staff management: different staff at different branches with appropriate permissions
- Integration path to delivery platforms or POS (Phase 3+)

**Success Metric:** "I can see that the birthday campaign brought back 40 customers this month who hadn't visited in 2 months."

## Persona 3 — The Beauty and Wellness Business

**Business Type:** Salon, Spa, or Nail Studio  
**Size:** 1–5 locations, 3–15 staff  
**Monthly Customers:** 200–1,500  
**Current Loyalty:** Stamp card or appointment booking app loyalty  
**Tech Sophistication:** Medium  
**Monthly Loyalty Budget:** 1,000–2,500 THB  

**Goals:**
- Fill appointment slots during slow periods
- Retain high-value clients (clients who spend 3,000+ THB per visit)
- Reduce no-shows and last-minute cancellations
- Build a VIP tier that rewards their best clients

**Pain Points:**
- High-value clients are invisible in the current system — all members are treated equally
- No way to target "haven't visited in 60 days" with a personalised offer
- Staff forget to issue stamps, or issue them inconsistently
- Competitors are offering the same stamp card experience

**What They Need from OneMember:**
- Point-based system (not stamps) — spend more, earn more, reflecting client value
- Tiered loyalty potential: Bronze/Silver/Gold based on spend (Phase 2+)
- Campaign scheduling: "send a win-back offer if a client hasn't visited in 45 days"
- Clean member profiles: name, visit history, points balance — visible to reception staff

**Success Metric:** "My VIP clients are visiting 20% more often since we switched to points."

## Persona 4 — The Retail Shop

**Business Type:** Fashion, Lifestyle, or Gift Retail  
**Size:** 1–10 locations, 5–30 staff  
**Monthly Customers:** 500–3,000  
**Current Loyalty:** Brand loyalty card or none  
**Tech Sophistication:** Medium-High — uses POS, e-commerce or Instagram Shop  
**Monthly Loyalty Budget:** 2,000–5,000 THB  

**Goals:**
- Increase average transaction value (earn more points by spending more)
- Drive offline traffic from online channels (Instagram followers → in-store)
- Build a data asset: know who their best customers are and what they buy
- Seasonal campaigns: double points during sales, birthday discounts

**Pain Points:**
- Instagram followers and in-store customers are completely separate databases
- No way to measure whether an Instagram post drove in-store traffic
- Loyalty card sign-up requires filling in a paper form — high abandonment
- Enterprise loyalty platforms are out of budget

**What They Need from OneMember:**
- QR scan sign-up: Instagram bio → QR → points profile in 30 seconds
- Spend-based points: incentivise higher basket size
- Campaign management: double points weekends, seasonal specials
- Member export for CRM or email marketing integration (Phase 2+)
- Commerce integration: earn points on online orders (Phase 3+)

**Success Metric:** "We can see that 30% of new members this month came from our Instagram bio link."

## Persona 5 — The Boutique Hotel

**Business Type:** Hospitality — Boutique Hotel or Serviced Apartment  
**Size:** 1–3 properties, 10–50 staff  
**Monthly Customers:** 100–500 stays  
**Current Loyalty:** None, or manual VIP tracking in spreadsheets  
**Tech Sophistication:** Medium  
**Monthly Loyalty Budget:** 2,000–4,000 THB  

**Goals:**
- Increase direct bookings (reduce reliance on OTAs like Booking.com, Agoda)
- Reward returning guests and encourage direct repeat bookings
- Identify high-value guests (frequent visitors, high spend)
- Offer something hotel chains have that boutiques don't: a loyalty programme

**Pain Points:**
- All OTA bookings are anonymous — no direct relationship with the guest
- No affordable way to run a hotel loyalty programme
- Cannot send a "welcome back" email to a returning guest because they booked through Agoda
- Manual VIP tracking is inconsistent and staff-dependent

**What They Need from OneMember:**
- QR at check-in: guests join while waiting, staff award points manually for the stay
- Points for nights stayed: simple, transparent, easy to explain
- Staff can view guest history: "this is Mr. Smith's fourth visit — 850 points"
- Win-back campaign: "Haven't seen you in 6 months — here are 200 points to come back"
- Future: direct booking integration, OTA bridge (Phase 3+)

**Success Metric:** "Our repeat direct booking rate has increased from 15% to 25%."

## Persona 6 — The Enterprise Chain

**Business Type:** Multi-location enterprise (restaurant chain, retail chain, hotel group)  
**Size:** 20+ locations  
**Monthly Customers:** 10,000+  
**Current Loyalty:** Proprietary system, LINE, or a major enterprise platform  
**Tech Sophistication:** High — dedicated IT team  
**Monthly Loyalty Budget:** Custom enterprise contract  

**Goals:**
- Centralise loyalty data across all brands and locations
- Integrate with existing POS and CRM systems
- White-label the loyalty experience to match brand identity
- Gain access to OneMember's customer wallet and network effects
- Reduce the cost and complexity of their current loyalty platform

**Pain Points:**
- Current enterprise loyalty platforms are expensive and inflexible
- Integrating loyalty with their POS requires long implementation projects
- Their loyalty app has low adoption — customers don't bother downloading it
- They cannot reach customers who are already in a competitor's loyalty programme

**What They Need from OneMember:**
- Enterprise Bridge API (Phase 2+)
- White-label membership experience
- Multi-location analytics and campaign management
- Integration with existing POS, CRM, and data warehouses
- SLA-backed uptime guarantees

**Success Metric:** "We retired our legacy loyalty platform and saved 3M THB per year."
