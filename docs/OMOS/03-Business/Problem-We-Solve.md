## Purpose

This document articulates — plainly and specifically — the problem OneMember solves for merchants and customers. This is the foundation of all product and marketing communication.

---

## The Merchant Problem

### Problem 1: Invisible Customers

Most small merchants in Thailand do not know who their customers are.

A restaurant may serve 2,000 people a month. They know their regulars by face, perhaps by name. But they have no record of who visited last Thursday, who has been coming weekly for two years, who tried them once and never returned, or who is their highest-spending customer.

Without this knowledge, every marketing effort is guesswork. Every promotion goes to everyone equally, regardless of whether they need an incentive to return. Loyal customers are not recognised or rewarded. Churning customers are not caught.

**What OneMember solves:** A structured database of real customers, with visit history, transaction records, and points balances.

### Problem 2: No Affordable Loyalty Infrastructure

Enterprise loyalty platforms exist and they work. They are also priced for enterprises. A mid-sized loyalty platform costs 50,000–200,000 THB per month. Most small businesses in Thailand spend less than that on their entire marketing budget for the year.

DIY alternatives (LineOA, paper cards, basic QR apps) are cheap but generate no useful data and cannot be automated.

**What OneMember solves:** A professional loyalty platform at a price small businesses can afford (500–3,000 THB/month), with the features that actually drive repeat visits.

### Problem 3: No Way to Reach Churning Customers

When a regular customer stops coming, a merchant has no way to know it has happened until weeks or months pass. By then, the relationship may be over. And even if they recognise it, they have no way to reach that customer.

**What OneMember solves:** Automated win-back campaigns. If a member has not visited in 45 days, OneMember sends them a personalised message with an incentive to return.

### Problem 4: Fragmented Data

A merchant's customers exist in multiple disconnected databases:
- Paper records from walk-ins
- LineOA followers (not real customer data)
- Instagram followers (no purchase connection)
- Delivery platform customers (owned by the platform, not the merchant)

None of these connect. None of them give the merchant a complete picture of their customer base.

**What OneMember solves:** A single structured record per customer, with opt-in consent and full ownership by the merchant.

## The Customer Problem

### Problem 1: Too Many Cards, Too Little Value

The average Thai consumer who eats at restaurants, visits cafes, and shops regularly is carrying paper stamp cards for at least 5–8 merchants. Most of the cards are lost, damaged, or forgotten at home. Most programmes are abandoned before the first reward is earned.

The loyalty experience is not loyal — it is irritating.

**What OneMember solves:** A single QR-based identity. No card required. Balance is always available by scanning the merchant's QR or viewing the wallet (Phase 2).

### Problem 2: Signing Up is Too Hard

Most loyalty programmes require filling in a form with more fields than the customer is willing to complete while standing at a counter waiting to pay. Name, phone, email, date of birth, address, ID number — all mandatory, none convenient.

Friction in the sign-up flow means the majority of customers who would benefit from loyalty never join.

**What OneMember solves:** A join flow of 3 fields (name, phone, optional birthday) that completes in under 30 seconds. No app download. No email verification loop at the counter.

### Problem 3: No Central View

A customer who is a member of 8 loyalty programmes has no way to see their combined balance, check when their points expire, or know which merchant they are closest to earning a reward from.

**What OneMember solves (Phase 2):** The Customer Wallet — a single view of every loyalty membership, balance, and transaction history in one place.

### Problem 4: No Privacy Control

Most loyalty programmes share customer data with marketing platforms or, in the case of LINE OA, with LINE itself. Customers have no visibility into what is being shared or with whom.

**What OneMember solves:** Consent-based data collection. Customers can see exactly what each merchant has access to. Customers can revoke membership and request data deletion.

## Why Now

The timing of OneMember is driven by three converging forces:

1. **QR code adoption is normalised.** PromptPay, food delivery apps, and health pass QR codes during COVID have trained Thai consumers to scan QR codes without hesitation. The friction of the QR-based join flow is lower than it would have been five years ago.

2. **Digital loyalty is expected.** Consumers who experience professional loyalty programmes at chains (coffee shop apps, supermarket loyalty) now expect equivalent experiences from independent merchants. Paper stamp cards feel inadequate by comparison.

3. **Regulation is rising.** Thailand's PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act) creates both a compliance requirement and an opportunity. Merchants who handle customer data informally are exposed. OneMember's consent-based architecture makes compliance easier and builds customer trust.
