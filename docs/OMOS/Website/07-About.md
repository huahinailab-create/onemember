# 07 — About Page

> Job of this page: make a shop owner (and a partner, and one day an investor) *trust* us. Trust in this market = a real story, real faces, real commitment to small merchants — not corporate boilerplate.

## Structure & copy direction

### 1. Opening — the story
- **Headline:** *We started with a simple question: why do only big chains get loyal customers?*
- **Body (the founding story, ~150 words):** Every 7-Eleven knows its members. Every small café knows… whoever the owner happens to remember. The tools existed — but they were built for chains, priced for chains, and spoke English at businesses that speak Thai. OneMember began with one belief: **the corner shop deserves the same customer power as the chain across the street** — at a price a two-person shop can afford, in the language they actually speak, simple enough to run between customers. `[FOUNDER: personalize with the real founding moment — the specific shop, the specific conversation]`

### 2. Mission
> **Give every small merchant in Southeast Asia the loyalty power of a big chain — in ten minutes, on their own phone, in their own language.**

### 3. Vision
- **Headline:** *A region of shops that know their customers.*
- **Body:** 1,000 merchants, then a network: where being a member of one great local shop makes it easier to belong to the next (the Customer Wallet future, told without jargon or promises of dates). "We're building the loyalty layer of Southeast Asian street commerce."

### 4. Founder philosophy (the beliefs section — differentiating, quotable)
Five short credos, each 2 lines:
1. **Merchants first.** If a feature helps us but not the shop, we don't build it.
2. **Free must be real.** 100 members free, forever. A promise, not a funnel trick.
3. **Your data is yours.** Export it any day. We will never sell it or hold it hostage.
4. **Simple is respect.** A shop owner's time is her scarcest asset; every extra tap is a small theft.
5. **We show up.** In Thai, on LINE, in person. Software companies hide behind tickets; we're from the street.
