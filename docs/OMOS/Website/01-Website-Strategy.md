**One-line objective: convert visitors into merchants.** Every page, sentence and pixel is judged by one question — *does this move a shop owner closer to "Start Free"?*

---

## Mission of the website

Be OneMember's best salesperson: deliver the 10-minute street demo experience to merchants we will never meet — in Thai, on a phone, at 11pm when the shop finally closes and the owner has time to think about her business.

## Brand positioning

**OneMember is a merchant growth platform — not "another loyalty system."**

- Category we refuse: loyalty software / CRM / points app.
- Category we claim: **merchant growth** — "we help your regulars come back more often, and regulars are how small shops grow."
- Against delivery platforms: *"Delivery apps rent you customers. OneMember helps you keep yours."*
- Against paper cards: *"Paper remembers stamps. OneMember remembers people."*
- Against enterprise suites: *"Big-chain customer power, sized for a two-person shop."*
- Proof spine on every page: **10 minutes to launch · free up to 100 members · Thai-first · no hardware, no app installs.**

## Target audience (in priority order)

1. **Khun Nok — owner-operator** (café/salon/shop; P1 persona): browses on phone, in Thai, at night; decides alone; wants confidence, not specs.
2. **Khun Ploy — hired manager** (P2): needs shareable ammunition (one-pager, pricing clarity, FAQ answers) to convince an owner via LINE.
3. **Khun Chai — multi-branch owner** (P3): scans for credibility, references, Enterprise signals.
4. Secondary readers: partners/POS vendors, journalists, future investors, jobseekers — served, never prioritized.

## Conversion goals

**Primary:**
1. **Start Free** (app.onemember.co/register) — the one true CTA, everywhere.
2. **LINE chat with sales** — the Thai-native secondary close (LINE OA button).

**Secondary:**
3. Book a demo (calendar/LINE) · 4. Watch the 90-second demo video · 5. Industry page → segment-tailored Start Free · 6. Pricing page ROI moment · 7. Newsletter/LINE follow for not-yet-ready visitors · 8. Enterprise "Talk to us" lead.

**Anti-goals:** no gated PDFs, no email-first capture (Thai SMEs don't email), no dark patterns (fake timers, hidden pricing).

## Brand personality

**The successful shop-owner friend.** Someone who ran a shop, figured out regulars, and shows you how over coffee — generous, concrete, slightly playful, never corporate.

Five traits: **warm · practical · confident (never arrogant) · local (Thai-first, street-level) · honest** (free means free; we say what we don't do).

## Writing style

- **Outcomes, never software.** ❌ "Multi-tenant campaign engine with configurable earn rules" → ✅ "Your customers collect points automatically — you decide what a free coffee costs."
- Short sentences. One idea each. Grade-6 reading level in both languages.
- **Merchant numbers, not vendor numbers**: "10 minutes," "100 members free," "2 taps at the counter," "6 extra coffees a month pays for it."
- Every section ends with a door: a CTA, a link deeper, or a question answered.
- Thai is the primary language of persuasion; English is the primary language of credibility (partners, Enterprise, investors). Write Thai natively — never translate English marketing literally.

## Voice

First person plural ("we"), speaking to "you/your shop" (คุณ/ร้านของคุณ). We say: regulars, your customers, your shop, come back, grow. We never say: users, clients, solution, leverage, synergy, cutting-edge, AI-powered.

## Tone (situational)

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Hero, CTAs | Confident, energetic — "Your regulars, coming back more often." |
| Problem sections | Empathetic, knowing — we've stood at that quiet counter on a Tuesday |
| Pricing | Transparent, calm — no pressure, free is real |
| Legal/security/PDPA | Plain, serious, jargon-free |
| Errors/empty states | Kind, helpful (matches in-app tone already shipped) |
| Myanmar-facing content | Respectful, patient, partner-led framing |

## Measurement stance (previews §12/§13)

North-star metric: **visitor → Start Free rate**, then **Start Free → Launch Ready** (the website's promise must match the product's 10-minute reality — no overselling, or activation pays the price).
