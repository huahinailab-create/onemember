# 03 — Discovery Questions

> Framework: **LISTEN 70 / TALK 30.** Discovery is a conversation at the counter, not a survey. Weave these in naturally; capture answers in the CRM sheet the same day.
> Goal: by the end you can say her problem back in her own numbers — and she nods.

## The 3-question street qualifier (before anything else)

1. **"Do your repeat customers matter to your business?"** — everyone says yes; the *way* they say it tells you the pain level.
2. **"Roughly how many customers do you serve a day?"** — sizes the shop, feeds the [ROI math](./07-ROI-Calculator.md).
3. **"Is this your shop?"** — identifies P1 vs P2 instantly and politely.

If qualified → continue below. If not (see ICP red flags) → leave a leaflet, a compliment, and go.

---

## Full discovery framework (10–15 min, can span two visits)

### A. Current loyalty
- "Do you do anything today to bring customers back — stamp cards, discounts, anything?"
- "How's that working? What do you like / hate about it?"
- (If paper cards) "How many stamps do you think you give away twice because someone lost a card?"
- "How do you decide who gets a special deal?"

### B. Repeat customers
- "Of today's customers, how many could you greet by name?"
- "Who's your best customer? When did you last see them?" *(the silence after this question sells more than any slide)*
- "When someone stops coming, how would you know?"
- "What do you think a regular spends with you in a month?" → write it down, use in ROI.

### C. Marketing today
- "Where do new customers find you — walk past, Facebook, friends?"
- "What did you last spend money on to get customers? Did it work?"

### D. Facebook
- "Does the shop have a Facebook page? Who posts?"
- "When you post, what happens — likes from friends or actual customers walking in?"

### E. LINE
- "Do you have LINE OA or a customer LINE group?" 
- "What do you send them? How many read it?" *(Sets up: "imagine that message saying 'you're 2 stamps from a free coffee'.")*

### F. POS / how the counter works
- "How do you take payment — POS system, phone, cash drawer?" *(Watch a transaction if possible.)*
- "Does your POS do anything for regulars?" *(If POS loyalty exists and is used → tread carefully; if it exists and is ignored → why?)*
- Note for us: OneMember is POS-agnostic — Counter Mode runs beside anything.

### G. Customer database
- "If you wanted to message your 50 best customers tonight, could you?"
- "Where do customer names/numbers live today — notebook, LINE chat, nowhere?"
- "Whose customers are they if a staff member leaves?" *(salons especially)*

### H. Challenges (open the drawer)
- "What's the hardest part of running the shop right now?"
- "Which day of the week hurts most?"
- "If Tuesday could look like Saturday, what would that change for you?"

### I. Goals
- "Where do you want the shop to be in a year — second branch, same shop but fuller, sell it?"
- "What would 'this loyalty thing worked' look like to you in 3 months?" → *this sentence becomes the success criterion you follow up against.*

### J. Budget (indirect — never open with price)
- "What do you pay for [delivery apps / FB boosts / that POS] a month?" *(anchors that she already pays for customer acquisition)*
- "If regulars came back one extra time a month, what's that worth?" *(her own number becomes the budget)*
- Only then, if asked: "There's a free plan up to 100 members — you can prove it works before paying anything."

### K. Decision makers
- "If you liked this, is it your call alone?" 
- (P2) "What would your owner need to see — numbers, a one-pager, a quick LINE call with me?"
- (P3) "Would you want to try one branch first?"

### L. Next steps (never leave without one)
- Best: "Let's set it up right now — ten minutes, free." → [Demo Script](./05-10-Minute-Demo-Script.md) close.
- Good: "I'll send the demo video + one-pager on LINE tonight; I'm back on this street Thursday — 10 minutes then?"
- Minimum: LINE contact exchanged + specific day named. "I'll think about it" = you offer the next step, not her.
