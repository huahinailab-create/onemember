# 08 — Follow-Up Playbook

> Deals in this market are won in the follow-up, not the pitch. Two cadences: **A** for merchants who started a trial, **B** for interested-but-not-signed. Channel truth (GO-TO-MARKET-001 §8): **LINE first, phone second, in-person for moments that matter, Facebook to observe, email for invoices only.**

## Channel rules

| Channel | Use for | Rules |
|---|---|---|
| **LINE** | 90% of all follow-up | Message 10:00–11:30 or 14:00–16:30 (never during service rush); one idea per message; screenshots > paragraphs; voice notes fine once rapport exists |
| **Phone** | Day-21 conversion call; anything gone quiet >7 days | Call before 11:00; open with "2 minutes — good time?" |
| **In-person** | Poster delivery; staff training; day-30 decision; pilot reviews | Never ambush at rush hour; always bring something (printed poster, case sheet) |
| **Facebook** | Recon + light touch | Like/comment on the shop's posts genuinely; DM only if LINE unknown |
| **Email** | Invoices, terms — compliance surface | Never rely on it for persuasion |

---

## Cadence A — Trial started (goal: Launch Ready ≤ 7 days, decision at day 30)

**Day 1 (evening of signup) — LINE**
> "วันนี้เยี่ยมมากครับ! Your shop is live 🎉 Checklist link: [link]. Tomorrow just add your logo — 2 minutes. I'm here if anything's confusing."
Attach: photo of their signup moment / their poster preview. *(Their checklist ≈40% from the visit; name the single next step — mirrors the product's own next-action.)*

**Day 3 — LINE (+ short call if checklist stalled <60%)**
- Moving: "You're at 70% — just the poster left. Print shop nearby or want me to bring one Thursday?"
- Stalled: call. "Which step is stuck?" Fix it *with* them, not for them. Log friction → product feedback channel.

**Day 7 — In-person (the poster visit)**
Bring the printed poster + table tent. Confirm: poster displayed, staff can do the two taps, ≥10 founding members enrolled (staff + best regulars). Celebrate Launch Ready in person — photo of the poster on the counter (with permission: future case-study material).
*Checkpoint: not Launch Ready by day 7 → this account is at risk; diagnose honestly (wrong fit? busy week? confusion?).*

**Day 14 — LINE (numbers, not features)**
> "Two weeks: 34 members, 51 visits recorded 👏 Shops your size usually hit 60 members by day 30 — the poster + a cashier prompt does it. One tip: [segment quick-win from 04]."

**Day 21 — Phone (the conversion conversation — trial ends day 30)**
Not a pressure call — an ROI review: her real dashboard numbers vs. the break-even napkin from the demo. "At 40 members and ~20 extra visits, this is already covering a Starter plan twice over. Trial ends next week — want me to help you pick the right plan, or start on Free?"

**Day 30 — In-person or LINE (decision day)**
- Converts → congratulate, set the monthly success touch, **ask for one referral** ("which owner do you know who needs this?").
- Chooses Free → equally warm: "100 members will come faster than you think." Log for the 100-member upgrade moment.
- Lapses (no activity, no decision) → one honest message: "No hard feelings — can I ask what didn't click? It helps us fix it." Answers here are gold; log verbatim.

## Cadence B — Interested, not signed (goal: get to a demo/decision, then stop)

**Day 1 — LINE:** thanks + the 90-second demo video + one-pager. "I'm on your street Thursday — 10 minutes to set it up free?"
**Day 3 — LINE:** one segment-specific proof point (case study once we have them; meanwhile the pet-birthday / win-back hook for their industry).
**Day 7 — Phone:** direct and light: "Should I keep a spot for you this month or check back after [their stated event — new branch, high season]?" A real "not now" gets parked politely with a date.
**Day 14 — LINE:** the referral-flavored nudge: "Set up [nearby shop] this week — their poster's up. Want yours before the street fills up?" (Only if true. It usually becomes true fast.)
**Day 30 — Final touch, then recycle:** "Last note from me — free plan stays free, and I'm around every week. Coffee's on me when you're ready." Move to a monthly passive list (FB likes, occasional value tip). **Never more than 5 touches in 30 days** — persistence sells, pestering burns the street.

## Universal rules

1. **Every touch gives something** (tip, number, printed asset) — never "just checking in."
2. Log every touch in the CRM sheet same-day; next-touch date is never empty.
3. Response SLA: merchant messages get answered <2h during business hours (this is our support promise too — [09](./09-Pilot-Merchant-Program.md)).
4. Escalation: any trial merchant silent through Day 7 + Day 14 touches → founder/senior takes one personal attempt, then account is honestly parked.
5. When they go quiet, the *product* keeps talking: trial-ending emails fire automatically (built) — your LINE messages should land the day before those, not compete with them.
