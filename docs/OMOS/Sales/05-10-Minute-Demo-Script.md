# 05 — The 10-Minute Demo Script

> Memorize the beats, not the words. Thai at the counter; this script gives the intent — deliver it in your own natural phrasing.
> **Setup before you walk in:** demo merchant seeded (`onemember:demo-seed` data) on your phone, battery >50%, offline fallback = printed poster sample + screenshots album. If she allows her phone to be used for the signup portion — even better.

---

### Minute 0–1 — Opening (earn the ten minutes)
At a natural pause, to the owner:
> "สวัสดีครับ/ค่ะ — I work with coffee shops on this street. One question: of today's customers, how many will come back this week… and how would you know?"

Beat. Whatever she answers:
> "That's exactly the problem I show owners how to fix. Ten minutes, on my phone, nothing to install — may I?"

If busy: "When's your quiet time today? I'll come back at 14:30." (And do.)

### Minute 1–2 — Two discovery questions (aim the demo)
1. "Roughly how many customers a day?" 
2. "Do you do stamps or anything today?"

Now aim: paper-stamps café → lead with stamps + lost-card pain; restaurant → lead with win-back + storefront. (Full framework: [03](./03-Discovery-Questions.md) — but in a street demo, two questions is enough.)

### Minute 2–4 — Be the customer (the scan)
Hold up the printed sample poster:
> "This sits by your register. Watch — I'm a customer now."

Scan the QR with your phone → join page appears (in Thai) → join as "Somchai" live.
> "Fifteen seconds. No app download. He's your member now — that's the whole thing your customer ever does."

### Minute 4–6 — Be the cashier (Counter Mode)
Switch to Counter Mode:
> "Now I'm your staff. Somchai buys a latte —" search name/phone → record purchase → points appear. "Two taps. Your cashier learns it in one coffee."

Redeem a reward for a seeded member:
> "…and when a regular hits her free drink: tap, done, she's grinning."

### Minute 6–7 — Be the owner (dashboard + win-back)
Open the dashboard:
> "And this is what YOU see tonight: members, points given today, and — this is my favorite —"

Show the win-back alert:
> "— these 12 people haven't visited in 30 days. One LINE message: 'we miss you, double stamps this week.' Paper cards can never tell you who disappeared."

### Minute 7–8 — Storefront + Launch Kit (pick ONE based on segment)
- Restaurant/fashion/retail → **Storefront**: "Your menu online, orders come to you — no app commission."
- Café/salon/spa → **Launch Kit**: open the poster page with the demo shop's own name/QR: "The system prints this for your shop — poster, counter card, even a one-page staff guide in Thai."

### Minute 8–9 — Campaigns & members (proof of depth, fast)
Flick through campaigns ("stamps or points — your rules"), a member profile ("visit history, birthday — it sends a birthday reward automatically"), then STOP demoing. More features ≠ more yes.

### Minute 9–10 — Close (the call to action)
> "Here's the honest part: **up to 100 members it's free. Forever.** No card. Setup is ten minutes and the app walks you through it — profile, first campaign, first reward, print the poster. Shall we set up *your* shop right now?"

Hand her the phone with the signup page open. **Silence. Let her type.**

- **Yes** → she signs up on the spot; the guided journey (launch checklist) takes over; you help until the checklist shows ~40%; promise the day-2 LINE check-in ([08](./08-Follow-Up-Playbook.md)). Before leaving: staff intro — "this is Counter Mode, two taps."
- **Hesitation** → surface it: "What would you want to be sure of first?" → [Objection Handling](./06-Objection-Handling.md).
- **Not the decider (P2)** → "Totally understand. Can we put 10 minutes with [owner] on LINE this week? Meanwhile, here's a one-pager built for exactly that conversation."

**Never leave without:** a signup, an appointment, or a LINE connection + named day.

---

### Demo hygiene rules
- Reset/reseed demo data every morning — never show yesterday's mess.
- Her segment's article open in a tab (Help Center quick starts) in case she asks "would this work for a [nail salon]?" — show her *her* industry guide.
- If anything breaks mid-demo: laugh, switch to the screenshots album, and note the bug the same hour (product team = your pit crew).
- Time yourself weekly. Over 12 minutes = you're feature-touring. Cut.
