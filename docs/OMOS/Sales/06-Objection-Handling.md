# 06 — Objection Handling

> Method for every objection: **Acknowledge → Reframe → Evidence → Advance.** Never argue; an argued merchant is a lost street. The answers below are intent, not word-for-word scripts — deliver in natural Thai.

---

## 1. "Too expensive." (แพงไป)
- **Acknowledge:** "Fair — every baht counts in a shop."
- **Reframe:** "That's why it starts at ฿0 — free up to 100 members, no card. You only ever pay after it's already proven with your own customers."
- **Evidence:** ROI napkin ([07](./07-ROI-Calculator.md)): "Your ticket's ~฿120. If just 5 regulars come back one extra time a month, that's ฿600/month — the plan pays for itself before breakfast." Compare anchor: "It's less than you tip the delivery app on two orders."
- **Advance:** "Start free today; we'll look at your numbers together in 30 days."

## 2. "We already use paper stamp cards." (ใช้บัตรสะสมแต้มกระดาษอยู่แล้ว)
- **Acknowledge:** "Great — you already believe in loyalty. That's the hard part."
- **Reframe:** "Paper rewards the customers who *remember the card*. It can't tell you who stopped coming — and that's where the money is."
- **Evidence:** "How many free coffees have you given to someone who 'lost' a full card? And when a regular disappears, paper stays silent. This shows you the 12 people who vanished last month." (Show win-back on the demo.)
- **Advance:** "Keep the paper cards running; let's set the digital one beside it for a month. Customers choose. Paper always loses."

## 3. "No time." (ไม่มีเวลา)
- **Acknowledge:** "I can see that — you haven't sat down since I walked in."
- **Reframe:** "That's exactly who this was built for. Setup is ten minutes *once*, and afterwards it works while you don't: birthday rewards send themselves, the poster enrolls customers by itself."
- **Evidence:** Show the launch checklist: "The app walks you through every step — most owners finish before their iced latte melts."
- **Advance:** "Give me the ten minutes now, or tell me your quiet hour and I'll come back and do it with you." (The second option closes more often.)

## 4. "My staff won't use it." (พนักงานไม่ใช้หรอก)
- **Acknowledge:** "Staff hate new systems — usually rightly."
- **Reframe:** "This isn't a system for them; it's two taps: find the customer, confirm. That's the whole training."
- **Evidence:** Do it live: "Time me." (4 seconds.) "And there's a one-page staff guide in Thai that prints from the app. If a new hire can use LINE, they can use this."
- **Advance:** "Let's show your cashier right now — if she frowns, I'll leave." (She never frowns; being consulted wins her over.)

## 5. "I don't really have repeat customers." (ไม่ค่อยมีลูกค้าประจำ)
- **Acknowledge:** "Maybe — some locations are pure foot traffic."
- **Reframe:** "Can I ask — is that a fact or a feeling? Most owners *feel* it and are wrong by half, because regulars are invisible without a list."
- **Evidence:** "That's actually the first thing this answers: within two weeks you'll know exactly — X regulars, Y one-timers. If it turns out you truly have none, I'll tell you to cancel myself." (If genuinely 90% tourists — qualify out honestly; see ICP red flags.)
- **Advance:** "Free plan, two weeks, let the data answer."

## 6. "Too complicated." (ดูยุ่งยาก)
- **Acknowledge:** "Software usually is."
- **Reframe:** "You never see 'software' — your customer scans a poster; your staff taps twice; you get a message with your numbers."
- **Evidence:** Hand her the phone: "Join as a customer yourself — fifteen seconds." Letting her *do* it dissolves this objection better than any answer. Point at the checklist: "and it tells you what to do next at every step, in Thai."
- **Advance:** "We'll set it up together right now so you never face a blank screen."

## 7. "I need my owner's approval." (ต้องถามเจ้าของก่อน) — the P2 manager
- **Acknowledge:** "Of course — it's their investment."
- **Reframe:** "Let me make you look good in that conversation." (The manager is now your teammate, not your gatekeeper.)
- **Evidence:** Give the owner one-pager + a 60-second summary she can forward on LINE; offer: "or a 10-minute LINE call with your owner at any hour — evenings fine."
- **Advance:** "When do you usually talk? I'll follow up the morning after." Log the owner as the real decision-maker in CRM.

## 8. "I need to ask my partner/husband/wife." (ต้องปรึกษาหุ้นส่วน/แฟนก่อน)
- **Acknowledge:** "As it should be — shops are family decisions."
- **Reframe:** "What do you think *they'll* ask? Let's prepare the answer together." (Surfaces the real objection — usually price or 'another gadget'.)
- **Evidence:** Answer the surfaced concern from this doc; send the demo video so the partner sees exactly what she saw.
- **Advance:** "Talk tonight; I'll LINE you tomorrow at 10. If they want, I'll demo again for both of you — ten minutes."

## Bonus objections you WILL hear

| Objection | Core response |
|---|---|
| "I already have LINE OA." | "Keep it — this gives your LINE something to say: 'you're 2 stamps from free' beats 'we exist'. They're teammates." |
| "My POS has loyalty." | "Is anyone using it? [Usually no.] Ours lives on the poster and the customer's phone, works beside any POS, and actually gets used." |
| "Is customer data safe? PDPA?" | "Consent is captured at join; the list is yours — export any time; we never sell or share it." *(Until DR-33 terms are ratified, do not improvise beyond this line.)* |
| "Will your company still exist next year?" | Honest founder answer + roadmap + "and your member list is exportable any day — you're never trapped." |
| "Give me a discount." | Protect pricing; give time instead: "I can't change the price, but the free tier means you pay ฿0 until it's proven." (Pilot cohort is the only structured free-Professional deal — [09](./09-Pilot-Merchant-Program.md).) |

## When to walk away (gracefully)

Two+ ICP red flags · pure price-shopper demanding discounts pre-trial · abusive to staff in front of you (they'll be worse to support) · "build me a custom feature first." Exit line: "I don't think we're the right fit for your shop today — but take the leaflet; things change." Log it; the street remembers politeness.
