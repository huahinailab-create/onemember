## Mission

Put OneMember on the counter of every small merchant who lives from repeat customers — starting with the first 100 in Thailand — by selling honestly, demonstrating live, and letting the product's ten-minute launch journey close the deal we open.

## Sales philosophy

1. **We sell repeat customers, not software.** The merchant does not want "a loyalty platform." She wants Tuesday afternoons as busy as Saturday. Every sentence you say should survive the question: *"does this help her regulars come back?"*
2. **The demo is the pitch.** OneMember's unfair advantage is that setup takes ten minutes. Never describe what you can show. Phone out, always.
3. **Free is a real answer.** The Free-100 tier is not a trick. If a merchant launches free and her poster sits on the counter, we won: that poster sells the street for us, and her 100th member sells the upgrade for us. Never pressure a Free choice — celebrate it.
4. **Honesty compounds in a small country.** Thai merchant communities talk (LINE groups, street neighbors, supplier gossip). One oversold merchant costs ten referrals. If OneMember is wrong for a shop, say so and leave a good impression.
5. **The product is the sales team's colleague.** The guided launch journey, checklist, and Launch Ready celebration were built to onboard merchants without us. Your job ends at "activated," not at "signed."

## Consultative selling — what it means here

- Diagnose before prescribing: run [Discovery](./03-Discovery-Questions.md) before any feature talk. The merchant must hear her own problem stated back in her own numbers ("about 60 customers a day, and you can name maybe 15").
- Prescribe modules, not the catalog: a nail salon gets stamps + birthday; a restaurant gets points + win-back + storefront. See [Industry Playbooks](./04-Industry-Playbooks.md). Selling everything is selling nothing.
- Quantify with her numbers: use the [ROI formulas](./07-ROI-Calculator.md) live, on paper, with her ticket size. A hand-written calculation on a napkin beats a slide.
- Advance, don't pressure: every conversation ends with a concrete next step *she* agreed to (see Follow-Up). No "think about it" exits — replace with "I'll message you the checklist link on LINE tonight, and drop by Thursday with the printed poster."

## Merchant-first mindset (rules we don't break)

- Demo on **her** phone whenever she'll allow it — possession is confidence.
- Never interrupt service. If customers walk in, step back, watch how the counter works (that's discovery too), resume after.
- Speak Thai, price in THB, message in LINE. Email is for invoices.
- Leave every shop better: a tip about their Google listing, a compliment passed to staff — sell or not.
- Her data is hers. Say it proactively: "your member list belongs to you, export any time."

## Typical sales process (9 stages — from GO-TO-MARKET-001 §6)

| Stage | Goal | Exit criteria | Tool |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Prospect | Get a conversation | Name, segment, contact in CRM sheet | Street cluster plan |
| 2. Qualify | Confirm fit + decision-maker | Persona tagged (P1–P4), ICP fit | [02](./02-Ideal-Customer-Profiles.md), [03](./03-Discovery-Questions.md) |
| 3. Demo | The "can I try it?" moment | Merchant asks to try, or a firm demo appointment with the decision-maker | [05](./05-10-Minute-Demo-Script.md) |
| 4. Trial | Signup on her phone, on the spot | Account created; launch checklist started | Product (MR-003 journey) |
| 5. Conversion | Paid plan or deliberate Free | Plan chosen at trial end | [06](./06-Objection-Handling.md), [07](./07-ROI-Calculator.md) |
| 6. Onboarding | Launch Ready 🎉 | Checklist 100%, poster physically displayed | Product + [08](./08-Follow-Up-Playbook.md) |
| 7. Success | Merchant states value unprompted | Monthly numbers touch delivered | CS cadence |
| 8. Renewal | Keep monthly churn <5% → <3% | Usage healthy (transactions weekly) | Health signals |
| 9. Referral | ≥25% of pipeline from merchants | Referral asked at every success touch | Referral offer |

## Expected timeline (calibrate your patience)

| Persona | Typical cycle |
|---|---|
| P1 Owner-operator (café, salon) | **Same visit to 1 week.** If she's the decider and the demo lands, sign up on the spot |
| P2 Hired manager | 1–3 weeks (one loop to the owner; arm the manager) |
| P3 Multi-branch owner | 2–6 weeks (pilot branch first) |
| P4 Ops manager (hotel/clinic) | 1–3 months (docs, permissions, terms questions) |

Trial to conversion adds up to 30 days (trial length). Do not try to shortcut the trial — merchants who feel the win-back alert fire once convert themselves.

## Daily activity expectations (full-time seller; founder pro-rates)

| Activity | Daily target |
|---|---|
| Street conversations (new prospects spoken to) | 10 |
| Full demos delivered | 3–4 |
| Trials started (signup completed in front of you) | 1–2 |
| Follow-ups sent (LINE, per [08](./08-Follow-Up-Playbook.md)) | every due one, before 19:00 |
| CRM sheet updated | 100%, same day, no exceptions |

Weekly rhythm: Mon plan the cluster + follow-up backlog · Tue–Fri street from 09:30 (after merchant opening rush) · Fri last hour = pipeline review against [KPIs](./10-Sales-KPIs.md).

**The one metric that matters for a new seller in week 1–4: demos per day.** Everything else follows the demo count.
