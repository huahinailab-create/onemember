## Purpose

This file prevents teams from re-proposing ideas that have already been evaluated and rejected. Every entry explains WHY the idea was rejected, so the reasoning is preserved.

An idea here is not permanently dead — circumstances change. But to re-open a rejected idea, the proposer must explain what has changed since it was rejected.

Format:
```
### REJ-[NNN] — [Title]
**Date Rejected:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Original Proposal:** [What was proposed]
**Why Rejected:** [The specific reasons — not "not now" but the actual objection]
**What Would Need to Change:** [Conditions under which this could be reconsidered]
```

## Rejected Ideas

### REJ-001 — Social Features (Reviews, Followers)

**Date Rejected:** 2026-07-02 (documented in CEO-Decisions era, pre-OMOS)  
**Original Proposal:** Add social features — merchant reviews, customer followers, community feeds — to the OneMember platform.  
**Why Rejected:** OneMember is not a social platform. Social features dilute the loyalty-commerce thesis, create moderation requirements, and compete with established social platforms where OneMember has no advantage. The loyalty-commerce flywheel does not require social features.  
**What Would Need to Change:** A demonstrated case that social features directly increase the North Star Metric (customer joins) or merchant retention. Not expected to arise.

---

### REJ-002 — Advertising Marketplace

**Date Rejected:** 2026-07-02  
**Original Proposal:** Allow brands to pay to show targeted ads to OneMember members based on their loyalty programme membership.  
**Why Rejected:** This requires treating customer data as a product, which violates CEO-005 (merchant data belongs to the merchant) and undermines customer trust. Any revenue from advertising would be offset by the trust damage.  
**What Would Need to Change:** This is not reconsidered without a fundamental change to the business model and explicit CEO approval after a trust/privacy impact assessment.

---

### REJ-003 — Cryptocurrency Payments

**Date Rejected:** 2026-07-02  
**Original Proposal:** Accept cryptocurrency payments in the Commerce module.  
**Why Rejected:** Cryptocurrency adoption among Thai SME merchants and their customers is negligible. Adding crypto payment infrastructure adds complexity, compliance risk (AMLA considerations), and conversion friction for no meaningful market benefit.  
**What Would Need to Change:** Significant increase in crypto payment adoption among Thai consumers and explicit merchant demand. Monitor market but do not revisit before Phase 4.
